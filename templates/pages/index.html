Scoring Methodology

Heat scores do not pursue absolute heat — they serve relative ranking among peer products. Core principle: only signals from the specific product SKU itself may be counted; brand heat must never substitute for product heat.

Total Score Structure

Heat Score = Sales Score (≤50) + Buzz Score (≤50), max 100.
Adjustable by category: Skincare/Efficacy 65/35 · Makeup 55/45 · Fragrance/New Makeup 45/55 · Luxury/Niche 40/60.
MVP stage uses 50/50.

Sales Score Formula

Sales Signal = Base × Platform Authority × Evidence Confidence × Time Decay
Countable signals: Bestseller / ranking / monthly sales·GMV / Sold Out·Restock / Hot·Trending tags / multi-channel distribution / authoritative retail endorsement
Evidence Confidence: explicit screenshot ×1.0 · platform official tag ×0.8 · third-party article mention ×0.6 · manual judgment ×0.4

Buzz Score Formula

Buzz Signal = Base × Platform Authority × Buzz Type Weight × Time Decay
Buzz three-tier weight: Organic ×1.0 > Editorial ×0.75 > Paid/Seeded ×0.45
Organic: user posts/comments/UGC/Reddit · Editorial: Allure/Vogue/NYMag/Cosmopolitan/Byrdie/WhoWhatWear · Paid: brand BGC/PR seeding/creator collab

Time Decay

0-30d ×1.0 · 31-90d ×0.8 · 91-180d ×0.6 · 181-365d ×0.4 · >1yr ×0.2
Long-term bestsellers may retain higher weight with continued evidence.

Display Score Tiers

90-98 Viral · 85-89 Trending · 80-84 Strong Interest · 75-79 Rising · 70-74 Niche Signal · 65-69 Low Confidence
Internal raw_score drives true ranking, never tampered; display_score capped at 98, floored at 65.

Core rule: A product cannot score high simply because its brand is hot. Only SKU-level sales/ranking/editorial/user-discussion/social/restock evidence counts. Brand heat without product-level evidence → background info only, no heat added.

Cross-panel consistency: Heat rankings and new-product radar use the same scoring formula and reporting-cycle data. The same product must have identical scores across both panels. The two panels differ only in product selection scope (Heat = market-wide TOP10 / Radar = last-4-week new launches); calculation is identical.
