Scoring Methodology

Heat scores do not pursue absolute heat — they serve relative ranking among peer products. Core principle: only signals from the specific product SKU itself may be counted; brand heat must never substitute for product heat.

Total Score Structure

Heat Score = Sales Score (≤50) + Buzz Score (≤50), max 100.
Adjustable by category: Skincare/Efficacy 65/35 · Makeup 55/45 · Fragrance/New Makeup 45/55 · Luxury/Niche 40/60.
MVP stage uses 50/50.

Sales Score Formula

Sales Signal = Base × Platform Authority × Evidence Confidence × Time Decay
Countable signals: Bestseller / ranking / monthly sales·GMV / Sold Out·Restock / Hot·Trending tags / multi-channel distribution / authoritative retail endorsement
Evidence Confidence: explicit screenshot ×1.0 · platform official tag ×0.8 · third-party article mention ×0.6 · manual judgment ×0.4

Buzz Score Formula

Buzz Signal = Base × Platform Authority × Buzz Type Weight × Time Decay
Buzz type weights: Organic ×1.0 > Editorial ×0.75 > Paid/Seeded ×0.45
Organic: user-generated notes/reviews/UGC/Reddit · Editorial: Allure/Vogue/NYMag/Cosmopolitan/Byrdie/WhoWhatWear · Paid: brand BGC/PR seeding/KOL collaborations

Time Decay

0-30 days ×1.0 · 31-90 days ×0.8 · 91-180 days ×0.6 · 181-365 days ×0.4 · >1 year ×0.2
Long-term Bestsellers may retain higher weight with sustained evidence support.

Display Score Range

90-98 Viral · 85-89 Trending · 80-84 Strong Interest · 75-79 Rising · 70-74 Niche Signal · 65-69 Low Confidence
Internal raw_score used for true ranking, not tampered; display_score capped at 98, floored at 65.

Core rule: A product cannot score high just because its brand is popular. Only the specific SKU itself must have sales/ranking/media/user discussion/social content/stock evidence to earn heat score. Brand heat alone without product-level evidence → background info only, no heat score.

Cross-section consistency: Heat Rankings and New Product Radar use the same scoring formula and reporting period data. The same product must have identical scores across both sections. The only difference is product selection scope (Heat = market-wide TOP 10 / Radar = recent launches).
